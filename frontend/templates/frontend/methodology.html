Methodology

1. Inflation Methodology — Laspeyres Fixed-Basket CPI

We calculate the CPI using the modified Laspeyres index, consistent with the methodology used by Sri Lanka's Department of Census and Statistics (CCPI base 2021=100) and the ILO/IMF Consumer Price Index Manual (2020).

CPI Formula

CPIt = (Σ (Pi,t × Qi,0)) / (Σ (Pi,0 × Qi,0)) × 100

  • Pi,t = Price of item i in period t (current)
  • Pi,0 = Price of item i in the base period
  • Qi,0 = Quantity (fixed weight) of item i in the base period

Inflation Rate Calculations

Year-on-Year (YoY): πt = ((CPIt − CPIt−12) / CPIt−12) × 100

Month-on-Month (MoM): πm = ((CPIt − CPIt−1) / CPIt−1) × 100

12-Month Moving Average: πMA = ((Avg CPI last 12 mo − Avg CPI previous 12 mo) / Avg CPI previous 12 mo) × 100

Core Inflation (Exclusion Method)

Following the CBSL paper "Revisiting Core Inflation in Sri Lanka" (Maheepala & Pathirage, December 2024), we exclude:

  • Volatile food items (fresh vegetables, fruits, coconut, eggs, chillies, onions)
  • Energy (electricity, gas, liquid fuels, solid fuels)
  • Transport fuels
  • Water supply

2. Elementary Index Compilation

Following DCS methodology, elementary indices use the geometric mean (Jevons formula):

PJevons = (Π Pi)(1/n)

Higher-level indices use the Laspeyres formula with expenditure weights from HIES 2019.

  • Fuel prices: CEYPETCO official website
